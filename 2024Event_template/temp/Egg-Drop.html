EGG DROP

What it’s all about?

Theme-“EGGSTRONAUTS : Make your egg covers land safely!”

Get ready to test your creativity and problem-solving skills! Egg Drop is an exciting challenge where participants must design a protective structure to prevent an egg from breaking when dropped from a height. But it's not just about safety—it's about innovation, efficiency, and fun! Join us in this thrilling experiment to explore the effects of velocity and acceleration on a falling object.
roadmap

Flow of Event

Introduction
Participants received a briefing on event rules, scoring criteria, and materials for crafting egg
shields.
Team Making/Registration
Teams of 4 members were formed and registered.
Shield Design Round
Teams crafted egg shields within a specified time, utilizing various materials.
Drop Test Round
Teams subjected their egg shields to a suspenseful freefall to test their effectiveness.
Prize Distribution
Prize will be awarded to top 3 teams.

Rules and Regulations

1. Only the materials provided by the organizers such as bubble wrap, straws, paper, and popcorn foam can be used.

2. Mobile phones are not allowed during the event.

3. All participants must follow the given instructions and maintain discipline.

4. It is mandatory to carry the institute ID card at all times by the participants.

5. The final decision rests with the jury, and no objections will be entertained.

6. The use of flammable materials is strictly prohibited.

7. Each team will be given 30 minutes to construct their egg lander.

8. Once time is up, the egg landers will be tested by dropping them from a standard height.

TESTING THE LADDER

The testing of lander will be done by dropping the package from a standard height. The following criteria will be considered :

1. There should be no cracks or leakage.

2. The duration of the fall will be recorded. The lander with minimum time of flight will be rewarded more points.

3. The minimum number of materials should be used.

4. Teams using fewer bidding points will be favoured.
